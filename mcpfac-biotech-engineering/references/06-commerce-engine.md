# Volume 6 — Enterprise B2B E-Commerce Engine (Business Rules, Order Processing, Customer Journey)

## E-Commerce Mission

An enterprise-grade B2B commerce platform serving research labs,
universities, pharmaceutical companies, biotech companies, government
institutions, distributors, and international wholesalers — supporting
both small research purchases and large institutional procurement while
staying modular and extensible.

## Business Model

Purchasing models: retail research orders, wholesale orders, distributor
orders, institutional procurement, laboratory supply contracts (future),
custom synthesis requests (future), research consultation requests, sample
requests (future).

## Product Types

Research peptides, growth hormones, research chemicals, reference
standards, laboratory reagents, laboratory kits, analytical standards,
custom products, digital documents, laboratory services.

## Product Lifecycle

Draft → Under Review → Approved → Published → Temporarily Unavailable →
Discontinued → Archived → Deleted. Products should not be physically
deleted unless explicitly required.

## Product Information Model

Company SKU, internal SKU, manufacturer code, product name, scientific
name, category, subcategory, description, research applications, CAS
number, molecular formula, molecular weight, purity, sequence, storage
temperature, solubility, appearance, packaging options, available sizes,
shelf life, research disclaimer, availability, lead time, shipping
restrictions, country restrictions, downloads, certificates, related
products.

## Product Images

Primary image, gallery images, zoom, 360° images (future), videos
(future), scientific diagrams, laboratory photos.

## Product Documents

Optional per product: COA, MSDS, HPLC report, technical datasheet,
research guide, certificate of origin, quality certificate, shipping
document. Each with version control.

> Consistent with earlier volumes: the platform stores/versions/serves
> these; their content is authored by the user's own lab/compliance
> sources, not generated by Claude.

## Pricing Engine

Price levels: retail, wholesale, distributor, institution, VIP,
promotional, volume, negotiated. Future regional pricing must be
supportable.

## Volume Pricing

Tiers, e.g. 1–9 / 10–49 / 50–99 / 100+ units. Display savings
automatically.

## Minimum Order Quantity

Per product: no minimum, fixed minimum, distributor minimum, institutional
minimum, country-specific minimum (future).

## Product Availability

In stock, low stock, made to order, back order, pre-order, unavailable,
discontinued.

## Product Search

Search by name, SKU, CAS number, molecular formula, sequence, keywords,
category, application, purity, research area. Autocomplete should support
scientific terms.

## Filtering

By category, purity, sequence length, molecular weight, availability,
storage condition, research application, price range, new products,
featured products.

## Product Comparison

Compare CAS number, purity, sequence, storage, formula, weight,
applications, availability, documentation, pricing.

## Related Products

Automatically show: alternative products, complementary products,
frequently bought together, recommended products, recently viewed,
featured products.

## Customer Registration

Registration types: individual researcher, university, laboratory,
company, distributor, institution. Capture: organization name,
department, research area, country, tax information (optional).

## Customer Types

Groups: retail, research, university, distributor, wholesale, government,
VIP — assigned automatically or manually. Pricing and permissions are
driven by customer group.

## Shopping Cart

Persistent cart, guest cart, saved cart, merge on login, bulk quantity
editing, remove items, save for later, cart notes, estimated-shipping
placeholder.

## Wishlist

Save products, move to cart, share wishlist, delete items, availability
notifications (future).

## Quotation Engine

Core workflow: add products to quote → adjust quantities → add notes →
optionally upload documents → submit quotation → receive PDF quotation →
track status → communicate with sales → convert to order.

**States:** Draft → Submitted → Under Review → Revised → Approved /
Rejected → Expired → Converted to Order.

## Bulk Order System

CSV upload (future), large quantity orders, distributor requests,
institutional requests, negotiated pricing, manual approval.

## Checkout

Shipping address, billing address, contact information, purchase order
number, payment method, order notes, review, confirmation.

## Payment Methods

Bank transfer, cryptocurrency, Alipay, WeChat Pay. Architecture should
allow future gateways without redesign.

## Order Workflow

Cart → Checkout → Pending → Confirmed → Processing → Quality Review →
Packed → Ready to Ship → Shipped → Delivered → Completed / Cancelled /
Returned (future).

## Order Management

Customers can: view orders, track orders, download invoice, download
documents, reorder, contact support, view timeline.

## Shipping Methods

FedEx, UPS, DHL, EMS, TNT, Aramex, China Post, freight forwarders. Each
shipment: carrier, tracking number, tracking URL, status, estimated
delivery.

## Invoice Engine

Branded invoices including: company info, customer info, products, prices,
tax, shipping, grand total, payment instructions, invoice number, invoice
date, due date.

## Download Center

Customers access: invoices, COA, MSDS, HPLC reports, research documents,
certificates. Searchable and filterable.

## Document Access

Permission tiers: public, registered customer, purchasing customer,
distributor, administrator. Sensitive research documents should require
authorization.

## Customer Dashboard

Widgets: orders, quotes, invoices, downloads, wishlist, support tickets,
messages, saved products, account summary, recent activity.

## Notifications

Notify on: registration, email verification, quote received, quote
approved, order confirmed, shipment created, shipment delivered, invoice
available, support reply, document updated.

## Support System

Customers can open tickets, attach files, reply, view status, escalate
(future), rate resolution.

## Returns

Architecture should reserve support for return requests, replacement
requests, damaged goods, and quality complaints, even if not enabled
initially.

## Research Compliance

Every product page should prominently display:

- Research Use Only
- Not intended for human consumption
- Not intended for diagnostic or therapeutic use unless explicitly stated
- Applicable shipping or regulatory restrictions where required

Do not generate unsupported scientific or medical claims about products.
This applies to all generated product copy, marketing content, and UI
text — efficacy claims, dosing implications, or therapeutic framing should
not be introduced even incidentally (e.g. in product descriptions,
taglines, or FAQ answers), and any such copy the user supplies should be
flagged rather than expanded on.

## Admin Product Management

Admins can: create, edit, duplicate, archive products; upload documents;
manage categories, pricing, inventory; publish/preview products.

## Inventory Management

Available quantity, reserved quantity, incoming stock, minimum threshold,
reorder alert, inventory history.

## Sales Analytics

Dashboard metrics: revenue, orders, quotes, top products, top categories,
downloads, customer growth, conversion rate, repeat customers, geographic
distribution.

## Email Workflows

Automatic emails: welcome, quote submission, quote approval, order
confirmation, shipment notification, invoice delivery, support updates,
password reset. Branded templates throughout.

## PDF Documents

Auto-generate: quotation PDF, invoice PDF, order summary, packing list,
product specification sheet (future). Consistent corporate layout.

## Business Rules

Every transaction enforces: product availability, minimum order quantity,
customer permissions, pricing rules, document permissions, inventory
validation, order integrity, audit logging.

## Security Rules

Never trust client-calculated values. Validate server-side: prices,
discounts, quantities, customer roles, document permissions, inventory,
order totals.

## Audit Events

Track: product updates, price changes, quote changes, order status
changes, invoice generation, document uploads, customer actions,
administrative actions.

## Performance Requirements

Support thousands of products, large document libraries, high search
volume, concurrent users, fast filtering, efficient pagination, optimized
queries.

## E-Commerce Testing

Test: product browsing, search, filtering, wishlist, comparison, cart,
checkout, quote workflow, invoice generation, downloads, customer
dashboard, permissions, document access.

## E-Commerce Completion Checklist

Business rules implemented, validation complete, permissions enforced,
pricing verified, inventory checked, documents linked, notifications
configured, audit events logged, responsive UI, tested, documented.

## Commerce Mode

When working in Commerce Mode:

1. Analyse the requested business feature.
2. Identify affected modules.
3. Define business rules.
4. Design frontend interactions.
5. Design backend workflows.
6. Define database changes if required.
7. Generate production-ready code.
8. Include tests.
9. Update documentation.
10. Verify compatibility with existing architecture.

## Future Commerce Readiness

Design so these can be added later without fundamental changes:
multi-currency, multi-language, multiple warehouses, distributor portals,
supplier portals, purchase order approval chains, regional pricing,
customer-specific catalogs, ERP integration, CRM integration,
subscription-based laboratory supplies, AI-powered recommendations,
laboratory procurement automation.
